Read and explain the post about Microservices Trade-Offs by Martin Fowler. (http://martinfowler.com/articles/microservice-trade-offs.html).

In a general context, he says that even though the microservices architectural style can be
a magnificient approach, like anything, it is not perfect and so with it some cons come along.

He explained that the pros of Microservices are:
- Facilitates the teamwork for large teams since it reinforces modular structure.
- Due to its independency, the simple services are easier to deploy hence, system failures are less likely to happen.
- A lot of technologies can be used and mixed for one application.

And that the cons were:
- Due to its distribution, systems are more complicated to program.
- A consequence of distributed systems is the difficulty to maintain a strong consistency.
- In order to manage lots of services that are redeployed regularly a mature operations team is needed.
